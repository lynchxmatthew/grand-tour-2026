01
Villefranche-sur-Mer
Côte d'Azur · French Riviera
Mon 29 Jun – Sun 5 Jul
6 nights
RivieraSwimmingHarbourCap FerratMarkets

We land in Nice and drive fifteen minutes east to Villefranche-sur-Mer — a deep natural harbour town that is one of the most beautiful and least pretentious places on the entire Riviera. A working harbour at dawn, the old citadel above the water, ochre and terracotta buildings stacked up the hillside, and one of the better sandy beaches on this stretch of coast directly in the town. Nice is fifteen minutes west, Menton is forty minutes east, and Cap Ferrat — one of the most beautiful peninsulas in Europe — is a thirty-minute walk around the headland.

Villefranche beach — a sandy arc in the town, calm water protected by the deep harbour, excellent for the kids.
Cap Ferrat day trip — walk the coastal path around the cape (45 mins each way), swim at Plage de Passable, lunch in Saint-Jean village.
Nice day trip — Cours Saleya flower and food market in the morning, old town for lunch, Promenade des Anglais in the evening.
Chapelle Saint-Pierre — Jean Cocteau decorated this tiny fishermen's chapel on the harbour front in 1957. Extraordinary and free to enter.
Èze village — medieval perched village 10 minutes east, one of the great coastal views in France from the ruined castle at the top.
Onward →Drive east along the Corniche Inférieure to Menton (~40 mins). Take the middle or upper corniche rather than the motorway — the views make the extra fifteen minutes worthwhile.

02
Menton
Côte d'Azur · French Riviera
Sun 5 Jul – Wed 8 Jul
3 nights
Old townMarketsItaly borderSwimmingGardens

Menton sits at the very eastern edge of France, pressed against the Italian border, and it shows — in the food, the architecture, the colour of the buildings stacked in amber and ochre above the sea. The warmest town in France, the least visited of the major Riviera stops, and arguably the most beautiful. Three nights is a concentrated dose: enough for the market, the old town, a day trip into Italy, and the extraordinary gardens before moving on to Nervi.

Marché du Bastion — every morning except Monday. The lemon products alone are worth the visit; the local cheese, charcuterie, and socca are extraordinary.
Old town and Rue Longue — climbing through the medieval quarter to the Basilica of Saint-Michel. The view from the cemetery above is one of the great Riviera panoramas.
Ventimiglia day trip — cross into Italy by train (~15 mins) for the Italian Riviera coast, excellent restaurants, and a taste of things to come.
Jardins de la Serre de la Madone — one of the great hidden gardens of the Riviera. Extraordinary plant collection, almost never crowded.
Onward →Drive west then north-west into Italy (~2hrs to Nervi/Genoa). The Via Aurelia coastal road through San Remo and Savona adds time but transforms the journey — consider it if the kids are settled.

03
Nervi, Genoa
Ligurian Riviera · Liguria
Wed 8 Jul – Mon 13 Jul
5 nights
PasseggiataHarbourCyclingGenoa day tripsParks

Nervi is a former fishing village absorbed into Genoa's eastern edge that has never felt like part of the city. It functions as a self-contained, elegant seaside neighbourhood — Liberty-era villas, a clifftop promenade above dramatic rocky coastline, a small harbour, and an extraordinary park system. The Passeggiata Anita Garibaldi is the centrepiece: 2km of clifftop walking path above the sea, considered one of the finest coastal promenades in Italy. The family will walk it every evening. Central Genoa — one of the great medieval cities in Europe — is twenty minutes by train, making it a day trip rather than your daily environment: a much better way to experience a dense, demanding city with two young children.

Passeggiata Anita Garibaldi — the clifftop promenade above the sea. Walk it every evening. The light at 8pm is extraordinary.
Genoa old city day trip — the UNESCO-listed caruggi (medieval alleyways), Mercato Orientale for lunch, the Palazzi dei Rolli. Take the train in (~20 mins) and back.
Parco di Nervi — three connected park villas with rose gardens, museums, and space for the kids to run freely. The modern art collection in Villa Grimaldi is excellent.
Boccadasse — a beautiful small fishing hamlet 15 minutes west along the coast by bike or bus. Coloured houses, good gelato, swimming off the pebble beach.
Cycling east from Nervi — the coastal road toward Camogli and Recco, and the inland climbs above Nervi toward Uscio, are excellent and traffic-free by early morning.
Onward →Drive or train west to Santa Margherita Ligure (~45 mins by car, ~30 mins by train). Camogli is directly on the route — worth a lunch stop if driving.

04
Santa Margherita Ligure
Ligurian Riviera · Liguria
Mon 13 Jul – Sat 18 Jul
5 nights
HarbourSwimmingPortofinoHikingRestaurants

Santa Margherita is the rare thing on the Italian Riviera: a genuinely beautiful town with real local life that hasn't been entirely consumed by tourism. The harbour is picture-perfect, the restaurants are excellent, and Portofino — one of the most beautiful villages in Italy — is a 20-minute boat ride away. From here the family operates on two speeds: boat trips and harbour swims on the easy days, and serious hiking on the trails between Portofino and Camogli when the energy is high.

Portofino by boat — the correct way to arrive. Walk the village, swim off the rocks at Paraggi on the way back.
Portofino headland hike — through to San Fruttuoso abbey, reachable only on foot or by sea. One of the great Ligurian walks.
Camogli by train — 15 minutes west, a proper fishing village painted in trompe-l'œil, excellent Friday market.
Trofie al pesto — the Ligurian original. Order it at every opportunity this week.
Onward →Drive direct to Lago d'Iseo (~3hrs via A7 and A35). A straightforward motorway run — save the energy for the lake. If time allows, exit at Brescia for a quick espresso in one of Italy's great underrated piazzas.

05
Lago d'Iseo
Tavernola Bergamasca · Lombardy
Sat 18 Jul – Thu 23 Jul
5 nights
Lake swimmingCyclingHikingFranciacortaMonte Isola

Lake Iseo is the least visited of the major Italian lakes and, in many ways, the most authentically itself. Tavernola Bergamasca sits on the western shore — a small village of working lakeside character, with a cement works that has operated since the 19th century and a local life completely untouched by the tourism that has transformed Como and Garda. The lake is dominated by Monte Isola, the largest lake island in southern Europe, rising 600m from the water and inhabited by a few hundred fishermen and their families. The swimming is excellent, the cycling into the hills behind the shore and into the Franciacorta wine country to the south is outstanding, and the whole area has a quiet, genuine quality that rewards slow attention.

Monte Isola by ferry — take the boat from Tavernola or Sulzano to the island, hire a bike, and circle the entire perimeter. No cars permitted. Extraordinary views in every direction.
Lake swimming off the rocks — the western shore below Tavernola has clear, calm water directly accessible from the village. The kids will be in within minutes of arrival.
Franciacorta wine country — the hills south of the lake produce one of Italy's finest sparkling wines. The cycling roads through the vineyards are exceptional and almost traffic-free.
Pisogne and the northern shore — the road around the northern tip of the lake passes through some of the most dramatic scenery, with cliffs dropping directly to the water.
Valle Camonica day trip — the valley above the northern lake contains the largest concentration of prehistoric rock art in the world, a UNESCO World Heritage site that the kids will find genuinely impressive.
Onward →Drive to Cortina d'Ampezzo (~3hrs via Brescia and the A4 east). A straightforward motorway run — consider a brief stop in Verona for lunch before turning north into the Dolomites.

06
Cortina d'Ampezzo
Dolomites · Veneto
Thu 23 Jul – Tue 28 Jul
5 nights
DolomitesHikingCyclingAlpineSwimming

The Dolomites are unlike anywhere else on earth — rose-gold rock towers rising from dark pine forests, a landscape so improbable it feels invented. Cortina sits at the centre of it all, a beautiful valley town ringed by the most famous peaks in the range. Five nights gives the family time to go deep: a full day on the Tre Cime circuit, a long lake day at Misurina, a rest day in town, and still time for the legendary passes.

Tre Cime di Lavaredo circuit — the most iconic hike in the Dolomites. Manageable for both kids with an early start. Allow 4–5 hours.
Lago di Misurina — swimming at 1,756m. Extraordinary colour, easy to reach by car.
Passo Falzarego and Passo Giau — back-to-back legendary climbs. Some of the most famous roads in cycling.
Cortina corso at 6pm — join the passeggiata with an ice cream and nowhere to be.
Onward →Drive to Bormio via the Stelvio Pass — allow a full day. 2,757m, 48 hairpins. Stop at the summit. The descent into the Valtellina is spectacular.

Bormio
Valtellina · Lombardy
Tue 28 Jul – Sun 2 Aug
5 nights
StelvioThermal bathsAlpine townHikingFood

Bormio is a small alpine town that knows exactly what it is and does it brilliantly. Roman thermal baths, extraordinary mountain food, and roads radiating in every direction that make this one of the great cycling bases in the Alps. More genuinely local than Cortina, slower in pace, easier on everyone after the intensity of the Dolomites.

Bagni Nuovi or Bagni Vecchi — ancient Roman springs set into the mountainside. A non-negotiable afternoon for the whole family.
Passo dello Stelvio from the Bormio side — 21km at 7.5% average. One of the great climbs in world cycling.
Parco Nazionale dello Stelvio — excellent trails, wildflowers in late July, marmots everywhere.
Pizzoccheri della Valtellina — buckwheat pasta with cabbage, potato, and Casera cheese. Order it at least once.
Onward →Drive to Menaggio via the Valtellina valley and Como lakeside road (~2hrs). Stop in Lugano for lunch — Swiss-Italian city on the lake, excellent espresso.

08
Carate Urio
Lake Como · Lombardy
Sun 2 Aug – Sat 8 Aug
6 nights
Lake ComoFerriesSwimmingVillagesCycling

Carate Urio is a small lakeside village on the western shore of Lake Como, 8km south of Menaggio — quieter, more genuinely local, and with direct lake access from the village itself. The same ferry network connects to Varenna, Bellagio, and the rest of the lake, and the western shore road running north toward Menaggio and south toward Como is one of the most beautiful cycling roads in Italy. Six nights is the right measure here: this is a deliberate exhale between the intensity of Bormio and the drama of Zermatt. The pace is slow by design — a different village by ferry each day, lake swimming in the afternoon, long dinners by the water.

Ferry to Varenna — the most beautiful village on the lake. Walk the Via dell'Amore, swim off the rocks below the castle. 40 minutes from Carate Urio.
Bellagio at dusk — arrive by ferry in the evening after day-trippers have gone. The light on the lake at 8pm is extraordinary.
Cycling north to Menaggio and beyond — the western shore road is one of the great lakeside rides in Europe, with the option of crossing into Switzerland via Lugano.
Lake swimming from the village — Carate Urio has direct lakefront access, calm water, and far fewer people than the more famous Como villages.
Villa del Balbianello day trip — one of the great gardens of Lake Como, 20 minutes south by ferry. Recognisable from Casino Royale and Star Wars. Book ahead in August.
Onward →Drive to Täsch (~2.5hrs via Lugano and the Simplon Pass or Gotthard tunnel). Park at Täsch — cars are banned in Zermatt. Train into the village (~12 mins). Bike travels on the train easily.

Zermatt
Valais · Switzerland
Sat 8 Aug – Tue 11 Aug
3 nights
MatterhornCar-freeHikingAlpine railways

Zermatt exists for one reason: the Matterhorn. The village is car-free, beautiful, and unlike anywhere else — a working mountain town at 1,600m where the 4,478m pyramid that defines the Swiss Alps sits at the end of every street. Three nights: Gornergrat on day one, Five Lakes Walk on day two, a slower final day before Geneva.

Gornergrat Railway — 33 minutes to 3,089m, the Matterhorn filling the window the entire way. Book the first train for the clearest views.
Five Lakes Walk — a classic half-day hike passing five mountain lakes, each reflecting the Matterhorn. Both kids can do it with the right footwear.
Zermatt village at dusk — the main street clears of day-trippers and the Matterhorn turns gold.
Onward →Train back to Täsch, collect car, drive to Geneva (~2.5hrs via the Rhône valley). Arrive at your brother's for dinner.

Geneva
Lac Léman · Switzerland
Tue 11 Aug – Sun 16 Aug
5 nights
Family stayLake swimmingChamonixLocal lifeRest

Five nights with family. A real kitchen, kids with cousins, a local's view of the region, and the freedom to simply be somewhere without logistics. Lake Geneva is swimmable and beautiful. Chamonix is one hour away. Your brother's knowledge will unlock things no guidebook reaches. This is the trip's exhale.

Chamonix day trip — the Aiguille du Midi cable car to 3,842m. Mont Blanc fills the entire sky. A full day minimum.
Lake Geneva swimming at Genève-Plage or Bains des Pâquis — proper public swimming, local crowds, excellent.
Carouge neighbourhood — Geneva's bohemian quarter, excellent coffee, Saturday market.
One day led entirely by the kids — after seven weeks of adult-directed travel, this does more for family morale than almost anything else.
Onward →Drive to Annecy (~45 mins). One of the easiest transfers on the trip. Arrive in time for a swim before dinner.

Annecy
Haute-Savoie · France
Sun 16 Aug – Fri 21 Aug
5 nights
Lake swimmingMarketOld townCyclingCafés

Annecy is, by almost any measure, the most beautiful small city in France. The old town — canals, geranium-hung bridges, medieval arcades — sits at the edge of the cleanest lake in Europe. The Tuesday and Friday markets are among the finest in the country. Five nights allows the family to settle properly into Annecy's rhythm — and it tends to become everyone's favourite stop of the trip. Arrives on a Friday, which means the market on Saturday morning is the very first thing.

Lac d'Annecy swimming — Plage d'Albigny for the full family; Plage de Talloires by boat for a quieter cove on the far shore.
Saturday and Tuesday markets on the Canal du Thiou — arrive at 8am, buy breakfast, eat on the canal wall.
Col de la Forclaz — a beautiful climb above the lake with a paragliding launch at the top.
Old town at dusk — the crowds thin after 7pm and the light on the canals turns extraordinary.
Onward →Drive to Grenoble (~1.5hrs) via Chambéry — worth a 45-minute coffee stop before dropping into the city.

Grenoble
Isère · French Alps
Fri 21 Aug – Tue 25 Aug
4 nights
CyclingAlpe d'HuezMarketHikingUniversity city

A university city ringed by three mountain ranges with real energy, an excellent food market, and some of the most extraordinary cycling and hiking in France immediately accessible. Alpe d'Huez is 45 minutes away — the most mythologised climb in Tour de France history. Arrives on a Wednesday; the Les Halles Sainte-Claire market runs every morning except Monday, so the family hits it Thursday through Saturday.

Alpe d'Huez — 13.8km, 21 hairpins, each named after a Tour stage winner. The family drives up; the climb happens on the bike.
Les Halles Sainte-Claire — one of the finest covered markets in France. Every morning except Monday.
Bastille télécabine — oldest urban cable car in the world, rising from a city street to panoramic views of three mountain ranges.
Vercors plateau — spectacular limestone landscape 30 minutes west. The Gorges de la Bourne trail is excellent for families.
Onward →Drive south (~4.5hrs) via Montélimar for lunch, then through the Drôme Provençale before dropping down to Collioure. Allow a full day for this drive.

Collioure
Roussillon · Occitanie
Tue 25 Aug – Sat 29 Aug
4 nights
Catalan cultureMarketSwimmingArt historyWine

Collioure is where France becomes Catalonia — culturally, linguistically, and gastronomically. A fishing village where Matisse and Derain invented Fauvism in 1905. World-famous anchovies, excellent Collioure AOC wine, swimming off the castle rocks, and the Albères mountains behind for serious cycling into Spain. Arrives on a Sunday — the weekly market on Place du 18-Juin is the very first morning.

Anchovy tasting at Roque or Desclaux — family-run salting houses open to visitors. Buy a jar to carry into Spain.
Matisse and Derain walking trail — the town has marked the exact spots where each painting was made.
Sunday market on Place du 18-Juin — local producers, Catalan food, the best weekly market in the Roussillon.
Cycling over the Albères into Spain — beautiful, quiet roads crossing the border almost imperceptibly.
Onward →Drive into Spain via Le Perthus (~1hr to Girona). Consider a Cadaqués detour — 45 minutes off the direct route and one of the most beautiful villages on the Spanish coast.

Girona
Catalonia · Spain
Sat 29 Aug – Fri 4 Sep
6 nights
Old cityFood sceneCyclingMarketsCosta Brava

Girona is what happens when a city has everything Barcelona promises but hasn't been overwhelmed by its own success. Roman walls, an immense cathedral, a Jewish quarter among the best-preserved in Europe, a world-class food scene, and the spiritual home of professional road cycling. Six nights allows the family to use it as a proper base: day trips to the Costa Brava calas, to the volcanic Garrotxa, and into the city at an unhurried pace.

Mercat del Lleó — Girona's covered market. Saturday is the best day. The Catalan charcuterie counter is outstanding.
Old city walls walk — free, complete circuit, 360-degree views over the city and countryside.
Costa Brava day trip — Begur and the calas around Tamariu and Aiguablava. Some of the finest cove swimming in the Mediterranean.
La Garrotxa — ancient volcanoes and Romanesque villages 45 minutes inland. Extraordinary beech forest hiking.
Onward →Train to Barcelona (~40 mins) — fast, frequent, effortless. Leave the car in Girona. Barcelona's traffic and parking are not worth the effort.

Barcelona
Catalonia · Spain
Fri 4 Sep – Tue 8 Sep
4 nights
GaudíFoodArchitectureNeighbourhoodsBeaches

Four nights is the correct dose of Barcelona — enough to feel the city properly without the urban density exhausting the kids. By early September the worst summer crowds have thinned. The Sagrada Família is genuinely unlike anything else in the world. But the best of Barcelona is found at neighbourhood level: dinner in Gràcia, morning coffee in El Born, an afternoon on Barceloneta.

Sagrada Família — book tower access well in advance. The interior in morning light is one of the great architectural experiences in Europe.
Boqueria at 8am — before the crowds, when it's still a working market. Buy breakfast, eat standing at a counter stool.
Gràcia for dinner — the most local-feeling of Barcelona's central neighbourhoods. The plaças fill with residents every evening from 8pm.
Park Güell at opening — arrive when the gates open to get the mosaics and city view before the day-trippers arrive.
Onward →Flight Barcelona → Palma de Mallorca (~50 mins). Very cheap booked ahead. Collect hire car in Palma and head north toward Sóller or Pollença.

16
Majorca
Balearic Islands · Spain
Tue 8 Sep – Mon 14 Sep
6 nights
Serra TramuntanaCala swimmingCyclingVillagesMarkets

September Majorca is the trip's reward — crowds thinning, sea at its warmest (28°C), the golden late-summer light transforming everything. We stay in the north, in the Serra de Tramuntana UNESCO World Heritage mountain range that drops directly into the sea. Medieval villages, olive groves, dry-stone walls, and turquoise calas reached by steep paths through pine forests. Arrives Sunday — Pollença market is the following Sunday on departure day, so plan the morning accordingly.

Cala Tuent — one of the most beautiful and least accessible calas on the island. The drive in is half the experience.
Deià village — Robert Graves lived here. Exceptional village, excellent restaurants, unmissable swim at Cala Deià.
Puig Major — the highest point in Majorca. One of the great Balearic climbs.
Pollença Sunday market — one of the finest weekly markets in Spain. Arrive early for local olive oil, sobrasada, and almonds.
Onward →Baleària Lines ferry Palma → Dénia (~3.5hrs). Stop in Dénia for lunch — excellent arròs a banda — before driving south to Altea (~45 mins).

17
Altea
Costa Blanca · Alicante
Mon 14 Sep – Sat 19 Sep
5 nights
Old townBeachesArt colonyRestaurantsCycling

Altea is a small, genuinely beautiful town on a hill above the Costa Blanca — a white-domed church, cobbled lanes, bougainvillea, and a view over the sea that justifies the climb. An artists' colony for decades, which means a restaurant scene far more interesting than anything else on this stretch of coast. September here is warm, calm, and very lightly touristed.

Altea old town at golden hour — the blue-domed church against the evening sky. Walk up every evening without fail.
Peñón de Ifach day trip — the giant limestone rock at Calp (20 mins south). The hike passes through a tunnel blasted through the rock.
Jávea day trip — 30 minutes north. The best cala swimming on the Costa Blanca at Granadella beach. Arrive early, stay all day.
Local rice dishes — arròs amb fesols i naps is the regional speciality. Order it at lunch.
Onward →Drive or train to Valencia (~1.5hrs). The coast road north through Benidorm is worth one look, then on into the city.

18
Valencia
Comunitat Valenciana · Spain
Sat 19 Sep – Wed 23 Sep
4 nights
PaellaMercado CentralArchitectureBeachesRuzafa

Valencia is the perfect final chapter — a beautiful, walkable city still largely experienced on its own terms. In mid-September the heat has softened, the city is in full local swing, and the light is extraordinary. This is where paella was invented, and the Valencian version — cooked over orange wood, eaten at lunch — is an entirely different dish from anything encountered elsewhere. And then, after twelve weeks, a flight home.

Mercado Central — the finest covered market in Spain. Go every morning. The seafood counter is extraordinary.
Paella at La Pepica or Casa Roberto — cooked correctly, eaten near the beach at lunch. A ritual worth observing.
City of Arts and Sciences — Calatrava's complex is genuinely spectacular. The Oceanogràfic is a full half-day for the kids.
Ruzafa neighbourhood — the most interesting part of Valencia for cafés, restaurants, shops, and evening street life.
Onward →Flight Valencia → Melbourne. Home from one of the best possible places to end twelve weeks in Europe.
